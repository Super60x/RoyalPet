Resting/Laying Down Prompt:

A breathtaking 17th century Flemish Masters oil painting portrait 
of a border collie dog resting regally as a noble aristocrat.

POSE: [PET PICTURE] lying down relaxed on a large golden velvet cushion 
with tasseled corners, body horizontal and at rest, 
head held high and alert looking toward viewer, 
natural dog paws tucked underneath or extended forward, 
NO human hands, NO human arms, [PET PICTURE] anatomy only.

ATTIRE: luxurious deep crimson royal mantle draped over the 
dog's back and body, trimmed with white ermine fur with black 
spots, heavy gold chain necklace with medallion resting on chest, 
subtle pearl collar underneath, fabric pooling naturally 
around the cushion.

ANATOMY: preserve natural [PET PICTURE] paws exactly, 
four legs with natural paws tucked or extended forward, 
no humanoid limbs whatsoever, natural resting dog position.

LIGHTING: dramatic Rembrandt chiaroscuro, warm golden light 
from upper left illuminating the face and chest, 
deep rich shadows on right side and background, 
warm amber tones on fur.

BACKGROUND: very dark near-black background, deep walnut brown, 
subtle hints of draped velvet curtain in dark burgundy, 
no distracting elements, all focus on the subject.

COLOR PRESERVATION: black and white fur markings must remain 
exactly as the original dog — natural coat colors preserved.

STYLE: Jan van Eyck, Rembrandt van Rijn, 17th century 
Dutch Golden Age, oil on canvas, visible aged brushstrokes, 
canvas texture, crackled varnish effect, museum masterpiece 
quality, ultra detailed.

MOOD: regal, dignified, serene, timeless, aristocratic.


Negative prompt:

human hands, human arms, human fingers, humanoid limbs,
standing upright, sitting upright, cartoon, anime, blurry, 
watermark, modern clothing, photograph, bright background,
modern furniture, flat lighting
